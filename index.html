I got my PhD in Computer Science in 2017 at University of California, Davis. I was working in Prof. Jonathan Eisen’s lab at UC Davis Genome Center. My research is mostly graph analysis in biological systems such as structural role discovery and the clustering problem. Currently, I am working on microbial network analysis; finding the co-occurring microbes and predicting their functions. I also love teaching. My latest project is time-series network analysis to find microbial communities in the plant root system. I will soon be joining AncestryDNA team as a Genomics data scientist.
